Bhagat Singh

1907 - 1931

The Fearless Fighter

Bhagat Singh, born in Punjab in 1907, was a brave young man driven by a fervent desire to free India from British rule. Inspired by stories of courageous revolutionaries, he joined the fight for independence. With unwavering determination, Bhagat Singh fearlessly challenged the British authorities. Through his writings, speeches, and daring acts, he aimed to awaken the spirit of resistance in his fellow countrymen. On March 23, 1931, Bhagat Singh and his comrades carried out a protest at the Central Legislative Assembly, raising the slogan "Inquilab Zindabad" (Long Live the Revolution). Their actions led to their capture and subsequent sentencing to death.


Bhagat Singh faced his final moments with bravery, becoming a symbol of resistance and igniting the flames of freedom across the nation. His sacrifice continues to inspire generations, reminding us of the importance of courage and unwavering dedication to our principles. Today, we pay tribute to Bhagat Singh, the fearless patriot who gave his life for the cause of freedom. May his spirit live on in our hearts as we strive for a better future, guided by his example of unwavering love for our country.
